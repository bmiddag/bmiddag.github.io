About

Hi there, and welcome to my webpage! I am Bart Middag. I recently graduated from Ghent University as Master of Science in Computer Science Engineering. There, I was known for the extra mile I went for my projects to make them truly excellent and give them a creative touch whenever possible, both programming-wise and design-wise. As such, I am a developer who has acquired skills in various creative domains: a creative developer.

One domain that allows me to combine my creative skills is video game development. Since I was only 11 years old, I have been programming, writing, drawing and composing to develop my own video games. It is my passion, so I am very interested in putting these skills to use in a work environment, whether as a game programmer or something else entirely.

Hobbies

I like video games! As such, a lot of my hobbies are related to this domain. I play a lot of video games and try to create them myself. Many video games come with beautiful artwork and music that I like to appreciate even outside of the game. That's why another of my hobbies is reverse engineering: figuring out how games are built and extracting or converting content from previously unknown file formats for personal use.

Recently I have been working on a Unity3D-based tool called Raymap, which can open and view maps from various 3D games in Ubisoft's Rayman franchise. It can not only view the environments but also all of the animations (unused ones as well), lighting, AI scripts (in a custom scripting language developed for these games), graphs (which e.g. tell how an object should move), and more.
